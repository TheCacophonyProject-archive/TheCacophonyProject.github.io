Moore's law

What is Moore law and why does it matter?

Moore's law is the observation that, over the history of computing hardware, the number of transistors in a dense integrated circuit doubles approximately every two years.

  • Cost of data collection is falling dramatically
  • Cost of data storage is falling dramatically
  • Cost of analysing the data is falling dramatically
  • Cost of removing pests going down as we learn more about best ways to do it
  • Cost of monitoring traps going down
  • Cost of reporting going down
  • Cost of getting wide spread social involvement is going down

So any approaches using computers and data are likely to fall in price and increase in effectiveness over time. Things that sound ridiculous today will possibly be viable much sooner than you may think. Eg sensors cheap enough to sprinkle in the forest from a helicopter, quad copters that can hunt possums, mice etc.
