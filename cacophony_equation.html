Theory

The Cacophony Equation

A Cacophony occurs when the cost of pest eradication is less than the wiliness to pay for pest eradication/control

Cost of pest eradication

  • Cost of initial eradication (95% easy 100% expensive)
  • Cost of getting the last of the pests….
  • Cost of monitoring
  • Cost of maintaining

Willingness to pay

  • Get wealthier – not much we can do about that
  • Earn more from eradication than other land use options – PurePods – ecotourism.

Examples of how it works for

  • island sanctuaries (very low cost for maintenance)
  • fenced sanctuaries (very expensive upfront….)
  • open sanctuaries – pest control ….aim is to make The WildSide the leading example for NZ…….

First project is really targeting the expensive subjective process of monitoring progress….if you can’t measure it easily, objectively and inexpensively then there is little hope of understand what is and isn’t working. Anecdotal sorties that there used be lots of birds compared to a graph showing it crashing vrs recovering if trapping is maintained seems vastly more convincing

The simple measuring of objective sound data will give a time series that should be able to identify

  • where are birds dying off fastest (largest pest growth)
  • Where bird population is returning fastest
  • Which conservation techniques work best in different areas
  • Which areas have the most promise for conservation efforts
